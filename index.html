About me

I am a PhD student in Economics at Université Paris 1 Panthéon-Sorbonne. My research focuses on migration, labour markets, remittances, cultural change, and political economy in Latin America. I am particularly interested in how large migration shocks affect attitudes toward redistribution, social cohesion and local development.

Methodologically, I work with household surveys, administrative data and spatial information, combining causal inference tools with machine learning methods. My current projects focus on Venezuela–Colombia migration flows, internal displacement and ethnic/racial inequalities.

Research

  • Colorism, race and solidarity: heterogeneous effects of Venezuelan migration on attitudes toward welfare and redistribution.
